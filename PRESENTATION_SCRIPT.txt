  SECTION 1: THE STORY + PROBLEM + HOOK                         [0:00 — 1:30]
████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████

  ─── THE OPENING (Speak slowly with weight) ───

  "Imagine this.

  You're buying a phone online. You find a seller with 4.8 stars, 
  thousands of reviews, glowing feedback. You trust the rating. You pay.

  Two weeks later — no phone. The seller has vanished.

  You leave a 1-star review. You report them. But the damage is done.
  And here's the worst part — by the time you left that review, 
  200 other people had already been scammed. Because the rating... 
  still said 4.8 stars."

  [PAUSE — let it sink in for 2 seconds]

  ─── THE HOOK (Land it hard) ───

  "This isn't a hypothetical. In 2024, the FTC reported $12.5 billion 
  lost to online marketplace fraud. And the #1 enabler? Static rating 
  systems that don't understand TIME.

  A seller can farm 5-star reviews for three months, then exit scam 
  in week four. And the rating system? It still shows 4.8 stars. 
  Because a simple average doesn't decay. It doesn't detect patterns. 
  It doesn't understand that TRUST IS TEMPORAL."

  [PAUSE — transition voice to confident, solution-oriented]

  ─── THE BRIDGE ───

  "That's exactly the problem we set out to solve."

================================================================================
  SPEAKING TIPS
================================================================================

  • The OPENING STORY is your most important 60 seconds. 
    Speak SLOWLY. Make eye contact (or look at the camera). 
    Don't rush to the solution.

  • When transitioning to the demo, STOP TALKING for 2-3 seconds 
    and let the dashboard's visual impact land. Silence is powerful.

  • During real-time demos, say "WATCH" before the score changes. 
    This primes the audience to look at the right spot.

  • If something breaks during the demo — OWN IT. 
    Say: "That's the reality of real-time systems at work" and move on. 
    Never apologize for more than 3 seconds.

  • End STRONG. The last line they hear is the one they remember. 
    Deliver the closing line like you mean it.
